First 7 Things to Do After Getting Your Tesla Model Y Juniper

Just picked up your new Model Y Juniper? Here are the 7 most important things to do right away — from essential inspections to must-have accessories and hidden features most new owners miss.

Last updated: March 2026

1. Inspect Your Car at Delivery

Before you sign anything, do a thorough inspection. Tesla's quality control has improved with the Juniper, but issues still slip through. Check panel gaps (run your finger along door edges), look for paint imperfections under direct sunlight, open and close every door and the trunk, test all windows, and verify the touchscreen works. Take photos of any issues and note them on the delivery checklist before accepting.

💡 Pro tip: Bring a flashlight and a checklist. Do this inspection even if the delivery person seems impatient — you're spending $45,000+.

2. Set Up Your Tesla Account & App

Download the Tesla app and log in with the account you used to order. This is your digital key — you'll use it to lock/unlock, start the car, check charging status, and precondition the cabin. Enable PIN to Drive for security (go to Controls > Safety > PIN to Drive). Set up your driver profile with your preferred seat position, mirrors, and steering settings. The car will remember these and auto-adjust when your phone connects.

3. Install a Screen Protector Immediately

The Juniper's 15.4-inch touchscreen is the center of everything you do in this car. It will get scratched and smudged within days. Install a tempered glass screen protector before you start using it. The Spigen EZ Fit with alignment tray makes this foolproof. Also get one for the 8-inch rear screen if you have passengers.

💡 Pro tip: Do this BEFORE you start touching the screen. Clean it with the microfiber cloth that comes with the protector, apply in a dust-free environment.

4. Set Up Sentry Mode with an SSD

Sentry Mode records suspicious activity around your parked car. The built-in dashcam records while you drive. Both need external storage — Tesla doesn't include any. Get a Samsung T7 SSD (500GB is plenty), format it as exFAT on your computer, plug it into the USB-C port in the glovebox, and enable Sentry Mode in Controls > Safety.

💡 Pro tip: Don't use a cheap USB flash drive — they fail constantly from the continuous write cycles. An SSD is more reliable and faster.

5. Add Floor Mats

The stock carpet mats are garbage. Get all-weather floor mats before your first rainy day. 3D MAXpider Kagu is the gold standard, or 3W if you want to save money. Make sure they say "Juniper" or "2025-2026" — old Model Y mats don't fit the refreshed interior.

6. Learn the Hidden Features

The Model Y Juniper has features that aren't obvious on day one. Joe Mode (Controls > Safety) reduces chime volume by half — essential if you have sleeping kids. Camp Mode runs the AC/heat while parked. Dog Mode shows a message on screen telling passersby your pet is safe. The Juniper's ventilated front seats have their own controls in the climate menu — don't forget to use them in summer.

7. Plan Your Charging Routine

If you have a home charger (Level 2, 240V), set daily charging limit to 80% and schedule it for off-peak hours (usually after 9 PM). The car's battery management system works best when you keep it between 20-80% for daily driving. Only charge to 100% before road trips.

💡 Pro tip: Set a charging schedule in the Tesla app under Charging > Schedule. This saves money on electricity and reduces grid strain during peak hours.
