Tesla Model Y Juniper Delivery Checklist (2025–2026)

Use this checklist for any new Tesla delivery: Model 3, Model Y, Model S, Model X, or Cybertruck. Tesla build quality is better than in prior years, but delivery-day misses still happen. Inspect first, then accept.

Last updated: March 2026 · 10 min read

⚡ Quick Answer

When picking up your Tesla Model Y Juniper, check these areas before signing: exterior paint and panel gaps (check in direct sunlight), all glass for chips/scratches, wheels for curb damage, open every door/trunk/frunk for alignment, test all windows and mirrors, verify the touchscreen and cameras work, check seat movement and interior trim, and take photos of any issues. Note defects on the delivery paperwork before accepting.

Do This Before You Sign

Once you accept delivery, getting issues fixed can be slower and less flexible. Document defects on-site with photos, and escalate serious fit/finish or functional issues before final acceptance.

Exterior Inspection

  • Panel gaps: Even and consistent — compare left vs right and front vs rear
  • Paint quality: Check in direct and angled light for chips, scratches, sanding haze, or color mismatch
  • Glass: Windshield, roof glass, and side glass for chips, cracks, and optical distortion
  • Doors, frunk, trunk: All should open/close smoothly without rubbing or binding
  • Lighting: Headlights and taillights should sit straight with no trapped condensation
  • Wheels and tires: Inspect every wheel face and sidewall — no curb rash or shipping damage
  • Charge port: Door should open/close reliably and sit flush
  • Weather seals: Check all door seals, window trim for gaps, tears, or loose clips
  • Cameras: Confirm housings are secure and lenses are clean

Interior Inspection

  • Main display: No dead pixels, discoloration, or touch dead zones
  • Seats: Test all powered seat movements and verify each seatbelt
  • Trim and panels: Check dash, door cards, console, headliner for rattles or loose alignment
  • USB ports and wireless charging: Test with your own devices
  • HVAC: Run AC and heat at multiple fan speeds — listen for unusual noise
  • Windows, mirrors, steering: Verify all switches work correctly
  • Juniper-specific: Check rear display responsiveness and ambient light continuity

Functional + Delivery Checks

  • VIN matches delivery documents
  • Phone key setup — pair your phone and verify lock/unlock
  • Software version and LTE/Wi-Fi connectivity
  • Camera feeds and parking visualization
  • Wipers, washers, horn, and all exterior lights
  • Tire pressure check — confirm all tires near recommended PSI
  • Enable PIN to Drive and review Sentry settings

What to Bring to Delivery

  • Phone with Tesla app installed and logged in
  • Flashlight for paint and trim inspection
  • This checklist (saved offline or printed)
  • Camera ready for documenting defects
  • Insurance proof and ID

FAQ

Can I refuse delivery if there are major issues?
Yes. If defects are significant, you can refuse and request re-delivery after correction.

How long should inspection take?
Plan for 30-45 minutes. Bring your checklist and don't rush.

Anything specific for refreshed Model Y Juniper?
Yes. Pay extra attention to rear display behavior, ambient lighting continuity, and trim transitions introduced in the refresh.

What if I notice an issue after leaving?
Open a service request in the Tesla app immediately and include photos and delivery-day timestamps.
